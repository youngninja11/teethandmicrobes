Teeth & Microbes

Human Teeth :

We are all born without teeth. Teeth start growing out of the gums later. By the age of three, the child has a set of 20 teeth. This set of teeth is called milk teeth or temporary teeth. At the age of six, the child starts losing the milk teeth. As the milk teeth fall, new teeth grow in their places from the gums. This new set of teeth is called permanent teeth.

How can we keep our teeth clean and healthy ?

1. Brush regularly but not aggressively

2. Use fluoride

3. Floss once a day

4. See a dentist regularly

5. Drink water instead of sugary drinks

6. Do not eat too much of sweets

7. Eat a well-balanced diet.

Microbes :

Microbes are very small living things. They can be seen only through a microscope. Microbes are found everywhere.They can be found in air, water, soil, food and also inside and outside the bodies of plants, animals and humans. Some microbes are useful to us and some are harmful. Microbes that cause diseases are called germs.

Harmfull Microbes

1. Bacteria - Typhoid, Diphtheria, Pneumonia, Tuberculosis

2. Viruses - Measels, Polio, Small pox, Influenza (Flu), Common cold

3. Fungi- Ringworm, Athelete's foot

4. Protozoa - Malaria, Dysentery
